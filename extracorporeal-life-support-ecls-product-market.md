<p><p>Extracorporeal Life Support (ECLS) encompasses critical life-sustaining interventions, primarily through two main types: Venoarterial (VA) ECMO and Venovenous (VV) ECMO. VA ECMO is utilized for patients with severe cardiac and respiratory failure, providing oxygenation and circulatory support by draining blood from a vein and returning it to an artery. In contrast, VV ECMO primarily addresses respiratory failure, extracting venous blood for oxygenation and returning it to the venous system, thereby maintaining circulatory function. Both types are crucial in critical care settings.</p></p>
