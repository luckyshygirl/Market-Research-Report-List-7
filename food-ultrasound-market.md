<p><ul><li>High-frequency Low-intensity</li><li>Low-frequency High-intensity</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The food ultrasound market encompasses two main types: high-frequency low-intensity and low-frequency high-intensity ultrasound. High-frequency low-intensity ultrasound is primarily used for non-destructive testing and quality control, enhancing food properties without significant heating or alterations. In contrast, low-frequency high-intensity ultrasound is applied in processes such as emulsification, extraction, and homogenization, providing intense energy to facilitate chemical reactions and physical changes in food products. Both types actively contribute to food safety, quality improvement, and processing efficiency.</p></p>
