<p><p>Synthetic Aperture Sonar (SAS) systems are categorized into three market types based on frequency: low, medium, and high frequency. Low-frequency SAS is used for long-range applications, offering deeper penetration and broader coverage, ideal for seabed mapping. Medium-frequency systems balance range and resolution, suitable for underwater object detection and identification. High-frequency systems provide superior detail and resolution for close-range applications, such as inspecting underwater structures, but have limited range, making them effective for detailed imagery in shallow waters.</p></p>
