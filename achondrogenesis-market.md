<p><ul><li>Achondrogenesis Type IA</li><li>Achondrogenesis Type IB</li><li>Achondrogenesis Type II</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Achondrogenesis is classified into three main types based on genetic causes and clinical features. Type IA, caused by mutations in the COL2A1 gene, is characterized by severe skeletal dysplasia and short limbs. Type IB results from mutations in the SLC26A2 gene, leading to a similar but slightly different presentation. Type II, also known as Langer-Saldino type, is associated with mutations in the COL11A1 gene and features less severe skeletal malformations. Each type has unique implications for treatment and prognosis, influencing market dynamics.</p></p>
