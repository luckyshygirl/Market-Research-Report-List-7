<p><strong>The Fuel Cell for CHP Applications Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>PEMFC (Proton Exchange Membrane Fuel Cells)</li><li>MCFC (Molten Carbonate Fuel Cells)</li><li>SOFC (Solid Oxide Fuel Cells)</li><li>PAFC (Phosphoric Acid Fuel Cells)</li><li>Others</li></ul></p>
<p><p>The fuel cell market for Combined Heat and Power (CHP) applications includes several types: </p><p>1. **PEMFC**: Uses a proton-conducting membrane and operates at low temperatures, ideal for residential uses. </p><p>2. **MCFC**: Operates at high temperatures and uses a molten electrolyte, suitable for large-scale industrial applications. </p><p>3. **SOFC**: Also high-temperature, this type features a solid electrolyte, known for efficiency in stationary power generation. </p><p>4. **PAFC**: Utilizes phosphoric acid as an electrolyte, typically designed for mid-sized applications. </p><p>5. **Others**: Includes emerging technologies and hybrid systems.</p></p>
