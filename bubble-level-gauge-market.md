<p><p>The bubble level gauge market is divided into two main types: non-contact and contact types. Non-contact level gauges utilize advanced technologies, such as lasers or ultrasonic waves, to measure fluid levels without physical contact, making them ideal for hazardous environments. In contrast, contact type level gauges involve direct interaction with the fluid, relying on floats, capacitive sensors, or pressure measurements. Each type caters to specific applications, balancing accuracy, safety, and environmental considerations to meet diverse industry needs.</p></p>
