<p><p>Hematologic malignancies encompass various types of blood cancers, primarily categorized into leukemia, lymphoma, multiple myeloma, and others. Leukemia involves cancer of the blood-forming tissues, leading to an increase in abnormal white blood cells. Lymphoma affects the lymphatic system, with two main types: Hodgkin and non-Hodgkin lymphoma. Multiple myeloma arises from plasma cells, resulting in bone marrow damage and various complications. The "others" category includes rare blood cancers and conditions that do not fit neatly into these classifications.</p></p>
