<p><p>The Fast Curing Nitrile Butadiene Rubber (NBR) market is divided into two key types: Cold Curing and Hot Curing NBR. Cold Curing NBR involves a curing process that occurs at room temperature, making it suitable for applications requiring minimal processing heat. In contrast, Hot Curing NBR utilizes elevated temperatures to accelerate the curing process, enhancing the rubber's properties and performance. Both types are utilized in various applications, including automotive seals, gaskets, and hoses, where rapid curing is essential.</p></p>
