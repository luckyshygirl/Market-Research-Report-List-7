<p><p>Membrane wastewater treatment (WWT) is an advanced process that utilizes selective barriers to remove contaminants from water. Key market types include microfiltration (MF), which removes larger particles; ultrafiltration (UF), targeting smaller molecules and colloids; nanofiltration (NF), filtering divalent ions and larger monovalent ions; and reverse osmosis (RO), which effectively removes a broad range of contaminants, including dissolved salts. These technologies enhance water quality and enable resource recovery, driving their adoption in various industrial and municipal applications.</p></p>
