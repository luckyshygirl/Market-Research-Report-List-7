<p><p>The Deep Vein Thrombosis (DVT) device market primarily includes venous stents and intermittent pneumatic compression devices. Venous stents are implanted to maintain open veins, enhancing blood flow and reducing the risk of clot formation. Intermittent pneumatic compression devices apply cyclic pressure to the legs, promoting venous return and preventing blood stagnation, which can lead to DVT. Both types of devices play a crucial role in managing and preventing DVT, catering to different patient needs and treatment modalities.</p></p>
