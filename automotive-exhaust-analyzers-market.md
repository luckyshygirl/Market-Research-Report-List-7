<p><p>The automotive exhaust analyzers market comprises several technologies for measuring emissions. Non-Dispersive Infrared (NDIR) analyzers detect gases like CO2 and hydrocarbons through infrared light absorption, offering high accuracy. Flame Ionization Detectors (FID) measure hydrocarbons by ionizing them in a flame, providing sensitive detection of organic compounds. Chemiluminescence analyzers assess nitrogen oxides (NOx) by measuring light emitted during chemical reactions. Other market categories include diverse technologies such as gas chromatography and electrochemical sensors, catering to various automotive emission testing needs.</p></p>
